How to Play

Setup

  • Players: 2–6. Each player gets a cup and 5 dice.
  • Roll secretly under your cup; keep results hidden.
  • Choose a starting player (youngest or random).

Turn Structure

  1. Bid — Declare a quantity and a face (e.g., five 3s).
  2. Raise or Call — Next player must raise the quantity/face, or say “Liar!”.
  3. Reveal — On a Liar call, all cups lift and dice are shown.
  4. Resolve — If the bid was false, the bidder loses a die; otherwise the caller loses a die.
  5. New Round — Re-roll; the player who lost a die starts next.

Optional Rules

  • Wild 1s — 1s count as wilds (match any face).
  • Palifico — With one die left, 1s are not wild and faces can’t change for that round.
  • Exact Bid — Exact calls reward a bonus (house rule).

Quick Tips

  • Track total dice — odds change as dice are lost.
  • Raising quantity is safer than raising face.
  • Use timing and table talk to pressure opponents.

FAQ

Q: How many players can play Perudo?
A: 2–6 players is ideal, but you can play with more.

Q: How long does a game take?
A: Most games last 15–30 minutes depending on the number of players.

Q: Is bluffing necessary?
A: Absolutely — it’s at the heart of the game!
