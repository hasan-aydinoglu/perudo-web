Perudo — Where Bluff Meets Brilliance

In the dusty markets of South America, travelers once gathered around worn wooden tables, cups in hand, dice rattling like distant thunder. From these humble beginnings came Perudo — a game where truth is optional, courage is rewarded, and every glance might be a bluff.

Will you speak the truth? Or weave a lie so convincing that no one dares call it? Learn the rules in minutes — but spend a lifetime perfecting your strategy.

How to Play

Learn the core rules in minutes — no grids, just clear steps.

Setup

  • Players: 2–6. Each player gets a cup and 5 dice.
  • Roll secretly under the cup and keep results hidden.
  • Choose a starting player (youngest/random).

Objective

Be the last player with at least one die.

Turn Structure

  1. Bid — The starting player declares a quantity and a face (e.g., five 3s). Quantity counts all dice on the table.
  2. Raise or Call — Next player must:
    • Raise by increasing the quantity (e.g., five 4s → six 4s) or raising the face (five 4s → five 5s), or
    • say “Liar!” to challenge the previous bid.
  3. Reveal — On a Liar call, all cups lift and dice are revealed.
  4. Resolve — If the bid was false, the bidder loses one die. If the bid was true (enough matching dice), the caller loses one die.
  5. New Round — Everyone re‑rolls secretly. The player who lost a die starts the next bid.

Endgame

Players who lose all dice are out. Last player with dice wins the game.

Optional Rules

  • Wild 1s — 1s count as wilds (match any face) until a palifico round.
  • Palifico — When a player drops to one die, during that round 1s are not wild and the face cannot change (only quantity can be raised).
  • Exact Bid — If someone claims the exact count and is correct, they regain a lost die (or penalize another — choose your house rule).

Tips

  • Track the total dice in play — probabilities shift as dice are lost.
  • Use timing and table talk to pressure opponents.
  • Change your pace: quick bids can force mistakes.
  • When unsure, raising quantity is usually safer than raising face.

Quick Reference: Raise quantity or face • “Liar!” to challenge • Reveal • Loser of the challenge loses a die • Re‑roll.

Why it’s brilliant

Easy rules, deep reads. Perudo rewards psychology as much as probability.
